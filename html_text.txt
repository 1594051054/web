            <h3><a href="/book/if-on-a-winters-night-a-traveler-9780156439619"> If on a Winter's Night a Traveler </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Italo Calvino </p>
            <p class="blurb">For those with an amorous affair with books, <em>If on a Winter's Night a Traveler</em> may well be the ultimate love letter to the reader. Calvino's novel is a masterfully created, startlingly unique work of fiction. Told alternately in second- and third-person narratives, the book is a fascinating exploration of the relationship between the author and the reader &ndash; weaving together seemingly unrelated tales, all of which relate directly to you, the reader. At its core is an ingenious concept the likes of which could have only come from the unparalleled imagination of Calvino. By the time you reach its dazzling conclusion, you'll be wishing you could somehow read it again for the very first time.
            <br />
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/jeremy-g">Jeremy</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/infinite-jest-9780316066525" rel="powells-9780316066525"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780316066525.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Infinite Jest" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href=" http://com-test-web1.powells.com/book/infinite-jest-9780316066525"> Infinite Jest </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by David Foster Wallace </p>
            <p class="blurb"><em>Infinite Jest</em> is unique; it was bred in the optimism and new frontiersmanship of the dot-com 1990s but was simultaneously an early omen of where we are today. It looks into our present beyond what were only horizons when it was written: the tensions of a global economy, the opiate of on-demand entertainment, the near-impossible pursuit of greatness in a winner-take-all society. Tennis phenoms struggle in an absurdly demanding academy and recovering addicts search for something strong enough to help them through, all while a cadre of legless Quebecois assassins search for a movie so entertaining that they plan to use it as a weapon. At turns madcap and heart-wrenching, this is the tour-de-force novel of the forces that have shaped our new millennium and will likely continue shaping it for decades to come. </p>
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Tye</a></strong></div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/left-hand-of-darkness-9780441007318" rel="powells-9780441007318"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780441007318.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="The Left Hand of Darkness" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/left-hand-of-darkness-9780441007318"> The Left Hand of Darkness </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Ursula K. Le Guin </p>
            <p class="blurb">Not only is <em>The Left Hand of Darkness</em> a masterpiece of ideas, invention, and language, but it takes conventional assumptions about gender and grinds them into a fine, powdery dust. Published in 1969, the book won both the Hugo and Nebula Awards and went on to become one of the keystones of science fiction. It tells the story of an ethnologist sent to another planet, but it is Le Guin's powers of imagination that turn <em>The Left Hand of Darkness</em> into something truly transcendent.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/mary-jo-s">Mary Jo</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <br clear="all" />
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/lolita-9780679723165" rel="powells-9780679723165"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780679723165.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Lolita" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/lolita-9780679723165"> Lolita </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Vladimir Nabokov </p>
            <p class="blurb">Why should everyone read a book about a pedophile's obsessive and frankly gross relationship with a little girl? Because if you are a reader &mdash; a lover of words, puns, witticisms, metaphors, and allusions &mdash; <em>Lolita</em> is a literary masterpiece that can't be passed over in a fit of queasy morality. Humbert Humbert, the novel's unreliable narrator, knows that he's a despicable pervert and yet the reader can't help enjoying him as he surveys post-war America and little Lolita with the droll, cynical eye of a European expat adrift in a tawdry nation, and stuck irrevocably &mdash; and irredeemably &mdash; in the memory of an adolescent love affair. Please, ignore the critics: <em>Lolita</em> isn't a morality tale and it isn't a love story. It's an unabashed look at a deviant mind written in some of the most deft and beautiful English ever published.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Rhianna</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/mans-search-for-meaning-9780807014271" rel="powells-9780807014271"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780807014271.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Man's Search for Meaning" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/mans-search-for-meaning-9780807014271"> Man's Search for Meaning </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Viktor E. Frankl </p>
            <p class="blurb"><em>Man's Search for Meaning</em> is like nothing you've ever read before. The first half of the book depicts Dr. Frankl's four years losing everything in concentration camps &mdash; a description so hellish, it leaves you desolate. Shattered by his Holocaust experiences, Frankl struggles to survive after he is freed. In the second half of the book, Frankl shows how that period of his life informs and develops his theory of "logotherapy" &mdash; he asserts that life is about finding meaning, what is meaningful to each individual. As excruciating as his experiences are, Frankl's theory is full of love; he is able to find redemption for himself and others. This book is beautifully life-changing.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Dianah</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/maus-a-survivors-tale-9780394747231" rel="powells-9780394747231"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780394747231.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Maus" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/maus-a-survivors-tale-9780394747231"> Maus </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Art Spiegelman </p>
            <p class="blurb">The twofold brilliance of Art Spiegelman's groundbreaking, autobiographical <em>Maus</em> is the graphic novel's lack of sentimentality and Spiegelman's self-portrait as a secondhand Holocaust survivor. The Holocaust is a widely used trope in Jewish American writing and although Spiegelman treats the subject with the compassion and historical sensitivity it merits, <em>Maus</em> avoids the themes of victimization and historical exceptionalism that render much Holocaust literature precious and insulated from the present. Instead, Spiegelman gives his characters the dignity of fully fleshed, complicated personalities and shows &mdash; in sometimes painful and unappealing ways &mdash; how his parents' Holocaust seeped into his childhood and haunts his being.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Rhianna</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/never-let-me-go-9781400078776" rel="powells-9781400078776"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9781400078776.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Never Let Me Go" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/never-let-me-go-9781400078776"> Never Let Me Go </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Kazuo Ishiguro </p>
            <p class="blurb">This is the kind of book that captures you so completely you find yourself reading it at work with the book covering your keyboard, hoping no one notices but also not really caring if you get fired. It's a subtle sci-fi story about youth, freedom, and a lot of other good stuff &mdash; too much more about the plot might take something away from the magical, transformative experience of reading it. Instead, I will say that the honest way <em>Never Let Me Go</em> deals with love and disappointment makes it a book that anyone who ever plans to love another person should probably read immediately.
            <br />
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Lizzy</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/peoples-history-of-the-united-states-1492-present-9780060838652" rel="powells-9780060838652"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780060838652.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="A People's History of the United States" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/peoples-history-of-the-united-states-1492-present-9780060838652"> A People's History of the United States </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Howard Zinn </p>
            <p class="blurb">While some of the revelations contained within this classic by Howard Zinn have become familiar since the nearly 35 years after it was published (thanks in part to this book), it is to this day an astonishing and eye-opening read. Several revisions later, it remains a seminal work, in stark contrast to the whitewashed (pun intended) American history most of us learned by rote in school. It's regretful with Zinn's passing in 2010 that new revisions have ceased for future generations to discover.
            <br />
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/jen-c">Jen C.</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <br clear="all" />
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/phantom-tollbooth-9780394815008" rel="powells-9780394815008"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780394815008.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="The Phantom Tollbooth" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/phantom-tollbooth-9780394815008"> The Phantom Tollbooth </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Norton Juster </p>
            <p class="blurb"><em>The Phantom Tollbooth</em> is the story of Milo, a very bored boy who comes home one day to find a magical tollbooth in his room. When Milo drives his car through the tollbooth gate, he finds himself in the Lands Beyond, a country inhabited by living language in the forms of animals, magicians, royalty, mountains, seas, and cities. From Tock the Watchdog to the listless region of The Doldrums, Milo shakes off boredom as he pursues the kidnapped Princesses Rhyme and Reason and restores peace to the Lands (currently in the clutches of the warring princes, Azaz of Dictionopolis and the Mathemagician of Digitopolis, along with a pack of demons). What sets <em>The Phantom Tollbooth</em> apart from other wonderful swashbuckling middle-readers is that it's also about the transformative power of language: open a book (or drive through a "tollbooth") and even the dreariest day dissolves into the din and glory of adventure.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Rhianna</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/poems-9780374532369" rel="powells-9780374532369"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780374532369.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Poems" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/poems-9780374532369"> Poems</a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Elizabeth Bishop </p>
            <p class="blurb">Elizabeth Bishop's poetry is dearly loved amongst her fans but perhaps not as well-known as it should be; for one of America's towering talents of the 20th century, she is not read nearly as much as Eliot or Whitman, or even cummings. That may be in part because of her relatively slim output &mdash; this volume of all her poetic works clocks in at only 368 pages. But the care she took with her poetry is evident; every word is perfectly chosen, none wasted or missing. Her work is fiercely intelligent, poignant, surprising, plainspoken, and wrought from imagery both familiar and extraordinary. A must-read for anyone who is interested in poetry, language, or indeed literature at all, Bishop's <em>Poems</em> speaks deeply to what makes us human.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/jill-owens">Jill</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/slaughterhouse-five-or-the-childrens-crusade-a-duty-dance-with-death-9780385333849" rel="powells-9780385333849"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780385333849.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Slaughterhouse-Five" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/slaughterhouse-five-or-the-childrens-crusade-a-duty-dance-with-death-9780385333849"> Slaughterhouse-Five </a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Kurt Vonnegut </p>
            <p class="blurb">What Kurt Vonnegut set out to do was write a book about war, and in particular the firebombing of Dresden in World War II. What he ended up doing was writing clean <em>around</em> it &mdash; traveling in and out of time warps, bouncing on and off the earth, sometimes setting down on the planet Tralfamadore, millions of miles away from Dresden and millions of miles away from war. What he created was a masterpiece of satire in which every crazy, clever moment, every whimsical line, no matter how deceptively light, is imbued with the sorrow and the starkness of the atrocity Vonnegut himself witnessed in that very real war.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/gigi-l">Gigi</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <br clear="all" />
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/things-fall-apart-9780385474542" rel="powells-9780385474542"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780385474542.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Things Fall Apart" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/things-fall-apart-9780385474542">Things Fall Apart</a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Chinua Achebe </p>
            <p class="blurb">Before <em>Things Fall Apart</em> was published in 1958, few novels existed in English that depicted African life from the African perspective. And while the book has paved the way for countless authors since, Chinua Achebe's illuminating work remains a classic of modern African literature. Drawing on the history and customs passed down to him, Achebe tells the tale Okonkwo, a strong-willed member of a late-19th-century Nigerian village. As we follow Okonkwo's story, we get a glimpse of the intricacies of village life and the complex social structures that come into play. We then see the devastating effects of European colonization on the region and on Okonkwo himself, whose rise and fall become intertwined with the changing power dynamics. <em>Things Fall Apart</em> is essential reading for anyone who wants a more nuanced understanding of other ways of life, of culture clashes, of what being civilized really entails.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/renee-p">Renee</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/to-kill-a-mockingbird-9780061120084" rel="powells-9780061120084"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780061120084.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="To Kill a Mockingbird" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/to-kill-a-mockingbird-9780061120084">To Kill a Mockingbird</a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Harper Lee </p>
            <p class="blurb">While <em>To Kill a Mockingbird</em> is a favorite book of pretty much everyone who has read it, it's important to remember that it continues to be subversive and challenging to the status quo. The protagonist is a young girl named Scout and except for her father, all the main characters in the book are marginalized by the power structure of their town &mdash; a structure that still exists nearly everywhere &mdash; where wealthy white men control the lives of everyone else, and even the members of that group who want to use their status for something honorable, like Scout's father Atticus, cannot win against the flattening wave of that power. Until something about that structure really changes, this book will remain required reading for every person in America.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks">Lizzy</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/where-the-wild-things-are-9780060254926" rel="powells-9780060254926"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780060254926.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="Where the Wild Things Are" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/where-the-wild-things-are-9780060254926">Where the Wild Things Are</a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Maurice Sendak </p>
            <p class="blurb">We all hold our favorite childhood books dear, but there's a reason <em>Where the Wild Things Are</em> is one of the most beloved picture books of all time. Of course it's about Maurice Sendak's whimsy, his spare poetry, his imagination. Of course it's about his impeccably detailed illustrations, depicting the beauty of a night of wild rumpus and the elegant fiendishness of wild things who gnash their terrible teeth and roll their terrible eyes. But mostly I think it's because underneath the boundless (yet beautifully bounded) inventiveness of Sendak's world, we see &mdash; and remember &mdash; exactly what it is to be a child.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/gigi-l">Gigi</a></strong></p>
            </div>
            <div class="readbefore_box">
            <div class="readbefore_cover">
            <a href="/book/wind-up-bird-chronicle-9780679775430" rel="powells-9780679775430"><img src="http://covers.powells.com/9780679775430.jpg" style="border: 1px solid #a0a0a0;" alt="The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle" /></a></div>
            <h3><a href="/book/wind-up-bird-chronicle-9780679775430"> The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle</a></h3>
            <p class="author">by Haruki Murakami </p>
            <p class="blurb">Known for his beautiful, haunting, lyrical, and &mdash; at times &mdash; funny surrealistic stylings, Haruki Murakami is one of the most beloved Japanese authors in the Western world. Although infused with the pop culture of the West, his writing remains at its core firmly rooted in Japan. And as modern as his style is, his work draws upon the country's past while delving deep into the Japanese psyche. <em>The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle</em> is pure Murakami &mdash; a vast, enchanting mystery filled with dreamlike surrealism. Considered by many to be his best work, the novel tackles themes as varied as the nature of consciousness, romantic disappointment, and the lingering wounds of World War II. Readers will eagerly want to unravel this intricate, multi-layered tale.
            <strong>&ndash; <a href="/staff-picks/jen-c">Jen C.</a> </strong></p>
            </div>
